Blood tests measure antibodies to H. pylori, while ELISA tests specifically target antigens. The urea breath test detects H. pylori by analyzing the patient's breath after ingesting a urea solution. Stool tests identify H. pylori antigens in fecal matter. Additional methods may include endoscopic biopsies and imaging techniques, providing a comprehensive approach to diagnosing and managing peptic ulcers.</p></p>
